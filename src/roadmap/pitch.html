The Problem

The bottleneck
isn’t talent.

It’s finding the
right reader.

Research takes hours. Keeping voice consistent at scale is hard. But the deeper problem is this: most AI content is written by practitioners, for practitioners. It never reaches the founder who’s drowning in tools and just wants their afternoon back.

The Insight

The AI content world
speaks to engineers.

Her readers are
founders.

Non‑technical founders under $50M are Googling “why do my AI tools make me less productive” — not “RAG vs fine‑tuning.” The pipeline is built to find that person, write for that person, and never lose sight of them.

The System

A private editorial
command center.

From idea to draft: fully automated.
From draft to published: Sondra decides.
The Pipeline

Four stages.
Fully operational.

Research
Scans two ecosystems — AI practitioner channels and founder community forums — and only surfaces topics where something real in AI maps directly to pain the ICP is already feeling.
Drafting
A writing agent that knows her voice drafts the article. Hero image generates in parallel.
Review
Premium reading environment with a live SEO score and a “Writing For” card that keeps the reader visible while editing. Approve or send back with notes.
Published
One click to her site via API. From idea to internet.
The Editorial Agent

Not a chatbot.

A colleague.

Someone who reads r/Entrepreneur to know what founders are struggling with this week, and follows the AI practitioner world to know what’s actually shipping. Someone who can connect those two things and tell Sondra: here’s the topic, here’s the angle, here’s why it’s the right moment.
