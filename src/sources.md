# Research Sources

The research agent monitors two distinct ecosystems and cross-references them to find topics worth writing about.

**Signal sources** tell us what's happening in the AI world: new tools, new techniques, new costs, new patterns. These are practitioner-facing and often technical.

**Audience sources** tell us what Sondra's ideal customer (non-technical founders, see `src/audience.md`) is actually confused about, frustrated by, and Googling at 11pm.

The best topics sit at the **intersection** of these two: something real is happening in AI (signal), and it maps directly to a pain point the ICP is already feeling (audience). Writing about signals alone leads to practitioner content the ICP will never find. Writing about audience pain alone leads to generic business advice without real AI insight. The sweet spot combines both.

## Audience Sources (Primary)

These are where the ICP actually hangs out. When scanning for topics, these should carry heavier weight than signal sources because they reveal the language, pain, and questions the reader is already typing into Google.

### Reddit Communities

- **r/Entrepreneur** and **r/Entrepreneurs** — Founders venting about pressure, hiring, hype, tool sprawl. Goldmine for "AI anxiety" and "falling behind" content.
- **r/smallbusiness** — Owners of established businesses skeptical of AI fad. Strong "is this actually worth it" signal.
- **r/AiForSmallBusiness** — ICP directly asking practical questions in their own language. Read every post.
- **r/automation** — People describing specific workflow pain and asking for relief. The "digital intern" crowd.
- **r/startups** — Founder-perspective AI conversations, investor pressure, strategy questions.
- **r/Sidehustle** and **r/solopreneur** — Solo operators who feel the admin burden most acutely.

### LinkedIn

- Non-technical founder voices writing about AI adoption, tool fatigue, and practical implementation for small business. Look for posts with high engagement from non-technical audiences (comments full of "this is exactly what I needed" responses).
- Specific creators worth tracking: writers who translate AI into business plain-language for founder audiences, SMB operators sharing real AI wins and failures, consultants serving the same ICP Sondra serves.

### Podcasts and Communities

- Founder-focused podcasts where AI comes up organically (not AI-dedicated shows, those skew technical). Indie Hackers, My First Million, Startups for the Rest of Us.
- Small business owner forums on Facebook, Slack, and Discord where ICP members ask practical questions in their own language.

### Direct Signals From Sondra's Work

- Questions prospects ask on discovery calls. If three different prospects asked the same question this month, that is a topic.
- Objections that come up in sales conversations. Objections are content.
- The frustrations clients vent about at the start of meetings before the "real" agenda starts.

## Signal Sources (Secondary)

These tell us what is genuinely new or shifting in the AI world. Useful for surfacing "something real is happening here" moments, but must always be translated through the audience lens before becoming a topic. Raw signal content is not for the ICP.

- **David Shapiro** — Philosophy, design, and architecture of autonomous agents.
- **AI Jason (Jason Zhou)** — Practical agentic AI tutorials.
- **Brandon (aiwithbrandon)** — Business use cases of AI agents, closer to ICP-relevant than most technical channels.
- **Alejandro AO** — RAG chains and AI-driven applications.
- **Sam Witteveen** — Google Developer Expert, deep technical on LangChain.

## How to Use These Sources

The research agent should:

1. **Start with audience sources.** What is the ICP asking about this week? What language are they using? What pain is recurring?
2. **Scan signal sources** for new developments that might connect to audience pain.
3. **Look for intersections.** A shift in AI tool pricing (signal) maps to the ICP's "subscription graveyard" frustration (audience). That intersection is the topic.
4. **Always frame the topic in audience language** before proposing it. "AI Agent Observability in 2026" is a signal title. "Why You Can't Tell If Your AI Tools Are Actually Working" is the same topic, audience-framed.
5. **Reject topics that only work from the signal side.** If the topic only makes sense to someone who already understands the technical concept, it is not a viable topic for this blog.

## Sondra's Core Content Angle (Refined for ICP)

Topics should help non-technical founders navigate AI without needing to become technical. The intersection of:

1. **The mundane pain** they already feel (tool sprawl, admin burden, investor pressure, hype fatigue).
2. **What's actually happening in AI** that relates to that pain (new tools, new pricing, new capabilities, new failures).
3. **Sondra's pragmatic advisory voice** cutting through the noise with specific, concrete guidance.

The research agent should always evaluate findings through the lens: "Would a non-technical founder, reading this topic in the middle of a busy day, feel this was written FOR them or AT them?"
